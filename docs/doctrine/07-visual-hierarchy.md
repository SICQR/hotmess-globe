# Visual Hierarchy Doctrine

Locked Phil 2026-05-27 (post `profile_bump` ship, pre `globe_glow` ship).

## 1. Visual hierarchy rule (sacred)

> Monetisation may amplify atmosphere, but must never override relational truth.
> — Phil, 2026-05-27

Relationship signals **always** outrank monetisation signals.

| Tier | Signal | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | TRUSTED status | Earned, not bought. Top of the hierarchy. |
| 2 | Mutual boo | Reciprocal consent — a real bond. |
| 3 | Safety / care state | Wellbeing always reads first. |
| 4 | Verification | Identity assurance for the room. |
| 5 | Beacon liveness | Active presence (state, not money). |
| 6 | **Paid amplification** | Glow, bump, blast. Subordinate to 1-5. |

If a paid signal ever visually competes with a relationship signal, the paid signal is wrong and must be dimmed, layered beneath, or removed.

## 2. The "felt feature" principle

A feature is not shipped until users can perceive it:
- status changes
- identity shifts
- movement / progression
- trust changes

Latent systems (running in the database, invisible to the user) are not features. They are scaffolding.

## 3. Globe glow restraint clauses

`globe_glow` and any future ambient amplifier:

**Allowed:**
- slightly larger bloom radius
- softer persistence
- richer ambient aura
- smoother fade curves
- subtle pulse cadence (rare, slow, ≤ 1 cycle / 8s)

**Forbidden:**
- flashing
- giant halos
- opacity spikes
- constant animation
- map obstruction
- neon aggression
- competing with relationship signals

Compass: *"like someone carrying heat, not buying billboard space."*

## 4. Post-purchase tone

After a successful boost purchase, the confirmation is **one line, no feature explanation.**

| Boost | Felt copy |
|---|---|
| globe_glow | Your pulse is glowing tonight. |
| profile_bump | You're at the top of the field. |
| vibe_blast | Your vibe is going out. |
| incognito_week | You're off the grid. |
| extra_beacon_drop | Drop another. The room is listening. |
| highlighted_message | Your message stands out. |

No "boost activated", no "tap to learn more", no feature explainer. The user paid; the line tells them what they're now *carrying*, not what they bought.

## 5. Why this matters

HOTMESS is becoming an emotional operating system, not a pile of modules. The question is no longer "can the app do this?" — it's "should the user feel this?"

When in doubt, the relationship side wins.
