# D31 — HOTMESS Venue & Partner Power Doctrine

**The HOTMESS constitutional doctrine governing operator power. Defines what venues, promoters, founders, partners, moderators, care operators, archive custodians, and future `.hotmess` property operators are allowed to see, influence, control, and inherit.**

**Status:** Locked
**Written:** 2026-05-31
**Author:** Phil
**Inherited from:** D08, D15, D19, D20, D22, D24, D25, D28, D34, EXECUTION
**Inherited by:** all operator tooling, venue dashboards, founder properties, moderation systems, partner integrations, and future multi-property governance.

---

## §0. Sacred Power Rule

**No operator relationship may become architectural authority over a user.**

Venues matter.
Promoters matter.
Care partners matter.
Founders matter.

But none of them outrank doctrine.

No commercial relationship, cultural influence, sponsorship, spend level, founder proximity, venue status, or operational convenience may weaken:

- D20 identity separation
- D08 visibility rights
- D15 care protections
- D22 retention boundaries
- D24 anti-hierarchy rules
- or user agency.

The platform may federate power.
It may never privatise constitutional protection.

---

## §1. Core Thesis

HOTMESS is not a neutral utility layer.
It is also not a feudal platform.

Operators exist because:

- nightlife requires coordination
- events require logistics
- care requires humans
- moderation requires intervention
- archive systems require stewardship
- local scenes require cultural context
- future `.hotmess` properties require leadership

But operator power must remain:

- bounded
- auditable
- purpose-specific
- revocable
- non-portable
- doctrine-constrained
- incapable of silently mutating user standing.

D31 exists to prevent:

- venue aristocracy
- sponsor privilege systems
- moderator absolutism
- founder exceptionalism
- hidden trust hierarchies
- safety theatre
- property fragmentation
- and partner capture of the OS.

---

## §2. Operator Classes

All operator power must declare class and scope.

| Operator class | Example | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| **Venue Operator** | club, sauna, gym, bar | venue-specific logistics + beacon control |
| **Event Operator** | promoter, host | event-specific coordination |
| **Commerce Operator** | HOTMESS commerce staff | fulfilment + dispute operations |
| **Care Operator** | aftercare/support partner | consented care routing only |
| **Safety Operator** | escalation responder | active safety incidents only |
| **Moderator** | trust & safety staff | policy enforcement under audit |
| **Archive Custodian** | archive.hotmess stewardship | memory/curation only |
| **Property Operator** | founder of future `.hotmess` property | local atmosphere + operational governance |
| **System Operator** | core HOTMESS infra | constitutional maintenance |

No operator exists without:

- declared class
- declared jurisdiction
- declared retention rights
- declared escalation rights
- declared audit scope.

---

## §3. What Operators May Never Do

No operator may:

- view Legal Identity without lawful operational reason
- view Recovery Identity casually
- access trusted contacts outside active safety flow
- assign public trust
- create VIP trust hierarchies
- alter D24 weighting manually
- create blacklist portability between unrelated properties
- override off-grid state
- expose pseudonymous users publicly
- export user graphs
- create follower systems
- weaponise moderation for social politics
- monetise user behavioural trails
- create permanent nightlife dossiers
- bypass D22 retention
- force profile disclosure
- create “verified elite” systems
- weaken doctrine because a sponsor asked
- merge Presence + Legal identity for convenience

The constitutional boundary is global.
No property opt-out exists.

---

## §4. Visibility Boundaries

Operators receive only the minimum visibility required for their operational scope.

### §4.1 Venue Operators

May see:

- venue beacon activity
- aggregate attendance estimates
- active event state
- user Presence Identity where operationally necessary
- ticket validity state
- local moderation alerts tied to venue safety

May not see:

- Legal Identity
- trusted contacts
- Recovery Identity
- internal trust weighting
- cross-property movement
- private user graph data
- off-platform contact extraction

---

### §4.2 Care Operators

May see only:

- consented care state
- minimal contact path
- active support context
- safety escalation state where required

May never see:

- commerce history
- nightlife behavioural history
- hidden trust systems
- unnecessary Presence detail
- unrelated moderation history

Care is not intelligence gathering.

---

### §4.3 Property Operators

Property operators may shape:

- atmosphere
- editorial voice
- local onboarding texture
- local moderation emphasis
- event topology
- local care routing
- local beacon rendering

They may not change:

- doctrine
- constitutional rights
- trust weighting rules
- identity architecture
- retention law
- cross-property continuity grammar
- safety override authority.

Texture is local.
Architecture is constitutional.

---

## §5. Trust and Operator Separation

Operator familiarity does not equal platform trust.

Examples:

- a club owner cannot mark a user “trusted” globally
- a sponsor cannot create “premium members”
- a founder cannot whitelist friends into immunity
- a venue regular is not automatically higher-trust
- spending money does not increase constitutional standing
- a property operator cannot export local status platform-wide

D24 remains sovereign.

Trust may emerge from behaviour only.
Never from proximity to power.

---

## §6. Moderation Doctrine

Moderation is infrastructure, not performance.

Moderators may:

- freeze harmful activity
- remove dangerous content
- pause exchanges
- intervene in active safety situations
- enforce doctrine
- preserve evidentiary records within D22

Moderators may not:

- publicly shame users
- create social punishment systems
- reveal private evidence casually
- preserve unnecessary behavioural history
- create ideology-based faction control
- use moderation as cultural gatekeeping
- expose reports to operators without need

Every moderation action must be:

- attributable internally
- audit logged
- reversible where appropriate
- bounded by retention
- explainable against doctrine.

---

## §7. Federation and `.hotmess` Properties

Future properties inherit doctrine automatically.

Examples:

- `archive.hotmess`
- `berghain.hotmess`
- `london.hotmess`
- `care.hotmess`
- founder-operated properties
- city/operator-specific surfaces

Inheritance rules:

- D20 identity model is mandatory
- D24 anti-status rule is mandatory
- D22 retention envelope is mandatory
- D15 care protections are mandatory
- D08 visibility rights are mandatory
- D31 operator constraints are mandatory

A property may differ culturally.
It may not become constitutionally incompatible.

---

## §8. Emergency Authority

Emergency powers exist only for active risk.

Allowed:

- temporary freeze during active abuse
- SOS escalation
- venue safety alert
- event shutdown propagation
- fraud containment
- coordinated harassment interruption

Emergency authority must:

- expire
- log automatically
- remain reviewable
- avoid permanent side effects unless doctrine permits.

There is no permanent emergency state.

---

## §9. Auditability

Every privileged action must be auditable.

Required logging:

- operator identity
- action type
- target scope
- timestamp
- doctrine justification
- retention class
- escalation chain

Forbidden:

- invisible moderator edits
- silent trust manipulation
- hidden operator surveillance
- undeclared partner tooling
- unaudited data export

The system must always be able to answer:

“Who accessed this, why, and under which doctrine?”

---

## §10. Data and Retention Boundaries

Operators inherit D22.

Rules:

- operators see bounded windows, not permanent histories
- venue data decays
- care notes minimise aggressively
- moderation evidence expires when no longer required
- archive systems preserve atmosphere, not exploitability
- properties cannot create shadow retention systems

No operator may privately preserve nightlife memory beyond doctrine.

## §12. Instrumentation

Allowed aggregate instrumentation:

- moderation completion time
- venue beacon uptime
- event invalidation propagation
- care escalation completion
- audit-log completeness
- abuse-response timing
- property-level operational health

Forbidden instrumentation:

- operator popularity scoring
- moderator productivity ranking based on punishment volume
- venue prestige ranking from behavioural extraction
- partner access to user behavioural analytics
- founder social graph analytics

Operators optimise safety and continuity.
Not extraction.

---

## §13. Acceptance Test

A build fails D31 if:

1. any operator can silently alter trust weighting;
2. any venue or partner can access Legal, Recovery, or unrelated Safety identity;
3. a property can override constitutional doctrine;
4. moderation actions are unaudited;
5. VIP or sponsor privilege changes user standing;
6. operator tooling enables behavioural surveillance;
7. off-grid users can be exposed through operator systems;
8. partner integrations export user graphs or nightlife trails;
9. emergency powers become persistent;
10. founder/operator relationships override doctrine.

---

## §14. Failure Mode If Violated

If D31 fails, HOTMESS stops being infrastructure and becomes nightlife feudalism.

Venues become sovereign.
Sponsors manufacture status.
Moderators become political actors.
Properties fragment into incompatible systems.
Pseudonymous users become second-class.
Care becomes intelligence gathering.
Trust becomes proximity to power.

At that point the substrate collapses.
