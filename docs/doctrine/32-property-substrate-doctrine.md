# D32 — HOTMESS Property Substrate Doctrine

**The HOTMESS constitutional doctrine governing federation, inheritance, atmosphere, property isolation, and continuity across all `.hotmess` worlds. Defines how many properties may exist while remaining one substrate.**

**Status:** Locked
**Written:** 2026-05-31
**Author:** Phil
**Inherited from:** D08, D15, D19, D20, D22, D24, D25, D28, D31, D34, EXECUTION
**Inherited by:** all future `.hotmess` properties, operator tooling, routing systems, atmosphere systems, archive systems, onboarding systems, and trajectory infrastructure.

---

## §0. Sacred Substrate Rule

**A user may move between radically different worlds while remaining protected by the same constitutional system.**

HOTMESS is not one property.
HOTMESS is one substrate.

Properties may differ culturally, atmospherically, geographically, operationally, and editorially.

They may not diverge constitutionally.

---

## §1. Core Thesis

Most platforms scale by flattening culture.
HOTMESS scales by federating atmosphere.

The system therefore separates:

- constitutional architecture
- from local texture.

Constitutional architecture includes:

- identity
- visibility
- retention
- care
- trust weighting
- operator limits
- trajectory continuity
- safety authority
- doctrine inheritance.

Local texture includes:

- tone
- atmosphere
- visual rendering
- onboarding texture
- local moderation emphasis
- cultural references
- beacon styling
- event topology
- geography
- local rituals.

This separation is the entire federation model.

---

## §2. Property Definition

A property is a doctrine-bound world running on the HOTMESS substrate.

Examples:

- `hotmess.com`
- `london.hotmess`
- `archive.hotmess`
- `care.hotmess`
- `berghain.hotmess`
- founder-operated properties
- city/operator-specific worlds
- future music/community/care properties

A property is NOT:

- a separate social network
- a white-label clone
- an isolated user database
- a franchise app
- a disconnected moderation regime

Properties inherit constitution automatically.

---

## §3. Constitutional Inheritance

Every property automatically inherits:

- D20 identity separation
- D08 visibility rights
- D15 care protections
- D22 retention envelopes
- D24 anti-status rules
- D25 messaging doctrine
- D28 reversal doctrine
- D31 operator limits
- D34 trajectory continuity
- EXECUTION supremacy.

No property opt-out exists.

A property may:

- reinterpret atmosphere
- reinterpret visual identity
- reinterpret onboarding language
- reinterpret pacing
- reinterpret editorial framing

It may not:

- create social castes
- weaken pseudonymity
- preserve nightlife surveillance
- expose Legal Identity
- create visible trust systems
- weaken off-grid protections
- bypass doctrine inheritance.

## §4. Property Registry Doctrine

All properties must declare themselves explicitly.

Required property definition:

- property id
- domain/subdomain
- operator class
- jurisdiction
- atmosphere profile
- moderation profile
- onboarding profile
- care routing profile
- beacon rendering profile
- retention overrides permitted by doctrine
- continuity policy
- emergency escalation path
- inheritance receipt

Suggested runtime structure:

- `/properties/<property-id>/property.config.ts`
- `property_registry`
- `property_capabilities`
- `property_atmosphere`
- `property_jurisdictions`

No undeclared properties.

---

## §5. Atmosphere Doctrine

Atmosphere is real.
It is not cosmetic.

Properties may alter:

- colour systems
- rendering density
- pacing
- typography
- sound identity
- onboarding texture
- beacon appearance
- language intensity
- archive feel
- motion profile
- ritual structure

But atmosphere must never silently alter:

- rights
- retention
- identity boundaries
- trust weighting
- visibility guarantees
- moderation protections
- recovery rights.

The emotional world may change.
The constitutional world may not.

---

## §6. Identity Across Properties

A user remains the same constitutional person across all properties.

Rules:

- Presence Identity may differ by property
- atmosphere context may differ by property
- visibility state may differ by property where doctrine permits
- continuity remains substrate-level
- Legal Identity remains global/private
- Recovery Identity remains substrate-level
- Safety protections remain substrate-level

A property may not force:

- re-verification for prestige
- public disclosure
- identity escalation
- cross-property exposure
- visible reputation carryover.

One substrate.
Multiple worlds.

---

## §7. Trajectory Continuity

Trajectory is substrate-wide.

A user moving between properties should feel:

- continuous
- remembered appropriately
- atmospherically transformed
- constitutionally stable.

Trajectory continuity means:

- pulse grammar remains consistent
- beacon semantics remain consistent
- care semantics remain consistent
- safety semantics remain consistent
- trust semantics remain consistent
- disappearance semantics remain consistent.

A beacon must mean the same thing everywhere.
A handoff must mean the same thing everywhere.
A safety escalation must mean the same thing everywhere.

Properties may reinterpret presentation.
They may not reinterpret ontology.

---

## §8. Property Isolation

Isolation exists for atmosphere and operational safety — not constitutional fragmentation.

Property isolation rules:

- local moderation context may remain local
- local beacons remain local unless propagated intentionally
- local atmosphere remains local
- local onboarding remains local
- local commerce rules may vary within doctrine bounds

But:

- trust weighting architecture remains shared
- identity architecture remains shared
- safety authority remains shared
- operator constraints remain shared
- auditability remains shared.

No property becomes sovereign.

---

## §9. Founder and Operator Properties

Founders may run their own `.hotmess` properties.

Rules:

- founders inherit D31 automatically
- founder proximity creates no constitutional privilege
- founders cannot bypass moderation
- founders cannot create hidden trust classes
- founders cannot export user graphs
- founders cannot weaken retention doctrine
- founders cannot force cultural conformity platform-wide

Founders shape worlds.
They do not own users.

---

## §10. Archive Properties

Archive properties are special.

They preserve:

- atmosphere
- memory
- trajectory fossils
- cultural continuity
- historical context
- scene memory.

They may not preserve:

- exploitability
- permanent nightlife surveillance
- reconstructive movement trails
- hidden behavioural dossiers.

Archive systems preserve feeling, not leverage.

D33 extends this doctrine.

---

## §11. Property Failure and Shutdown

Properties may fail.
The substrate must survive.

Rules:

- users retain substrate continuity
- Recovery Identity remains global
- Safety protections remain global
- unresolved disputes migrate safely
- archive transfer follows D22/D33
- property shutdown does not orphan users
- no operator may hold identity hostage.

A property ending is not a constitutional death.

---

## §12. Emergency Doctrine

Emergency authority remains substrate-level.

Examples:

- coordinated abuse
- legal injunction
- safety emergency
- fraud attack
- infrastructure compromise
- active stalking network

Property operators may escalate.
They may not permanently suspend constitutional rights outside doctrine.

Emergency authority must:

- log automatically
- decay automatically
- remain reviewable
- preserve minimum necessary continuity.

## §14. Retention Impact

Properties inherit D22.

Rules:

- local atmosphere may decay differently within doctrine
- local continuity windows may differ where allowed
- archive properties may preserve atmosphere longer
- evidentiary records remain bounded globally
- cross-property continuity must avoid reconstruction risk
- disappearance semantics remain enforceable substrate-wide.

No property may privately preserve nightlife history beyond doctrine.

---

## §15. Instrumentation

Allowed aggregate instrumentation:

- property health
- atmosphere engagement aggregate
- onboarding completion aggregate
- continuity movement aggregate
- cross-property transition aggregate
- moderation health
- care escalation health
- archive interaction aggregate

Forbidden instrumentation:

- cross-property behavioural dossiers
- founder/operator social graph extraction
- property prestige ranking from surveillance
- hidden recommendation hierarchies
- exportable nightlife movement timelines.

The substrate observes continuity.
It does not become omniscient.

---

## §16. Acceptance Test

A build fails D32 if:

1. a property can override constitutional doctrine;
2. identity architecture fragments between properties;
3. atmosphere silently changes rights or trust;
4. cross-property continuity becomes behavioural surveillance;
5. founders/operators gain hidden privilege;
6. properties create visible trust or status systems;
7. archive systems preserve exploitability;
8. shutdown of a property strands users;
9. property isolation breaks substrate-wide safety;
10. ontology diverges between properties.

---

## §17. Failure Mode If Violated

If D32 fails, HOTMESS collapses into disconnected fiefdoms.

Properties become incompatible.
Identity fragments.
Operators become sovereign.
Atmosphere mutates rights.
Continuity becomes surveillance.
Archives become leverage.
Trust becomes local politics.

At that point the substrate stops existing.
