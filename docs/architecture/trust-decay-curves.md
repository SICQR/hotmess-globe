# HOTMESS Trust Decay Curves

## Purpose

Define how trust:
- increases
- stabilizes
- decays
- freezes
- recovers

across HOTMESS.

Trust is operational confidence.

NOT reputation theater.

---

# Core Principle

Trust must:
- be earned slowly
- decay naturally
- collapse quickly under abuse
- recover cautiously
- remain contextual

Trust that never decays becomes corrupted authority.

---

# Trust Lifecycle

Trust moves through:

1. Seeded
2. Emerging
3. Stable
4. Trusted
5. High-trust
6. Restricted
7. Frozen

Trust is reversible.

No actor becomes permanently immune.

---

# Trust Bands

## Restricted
0–24

Characteristics:
- severe limits
- aggressive suppression
- strong cooldowns
- limited visibility

---

## Low
25–49

Characteristics:
- weak authority
- rapid decay
- requires corroboration
- low amplification tolerance

---

## Normal
50–69

Characteristics:
- standard visibility
- moderate persistence
- moderate decay

---

## Trusted
70–84

Characteristics:
- stronger stability
- slower decay
- higher confidence
- better persistence

---

## High-Trust
85–100

Characteristics:
- strongest operational confidence
- slower decay
- highest corroboration weight

Still reversible.

---

# Trust Inputs

Positive:
- accurate updates
- verified actions
- corroborated signals
- low dispute rate
- moderation stability
- consistency over time

Negative:
- misinformation
- spam
- harassment
- manipulation
- contradiction
- unresolved disputes
- abuse reports

---

# Trust Decay Principles

Trust decays when:
- inactivity persists
- contradictions increase
- spam rises
- reports accumulate
- stale behavior dominates
- manipulation patterns emerge

Trust decay should:
- be continuous
- be contextual
- vary by actor type

---

# Actor-Specific Decay

## Users
Decay faster.

Reason:
- behavior volatility
- low signal consistency

---

## Venues
Decay slower.

Reason:
- operational continuity
- physical persistence

---

## Promoters
Decay aggressively under saturation.

Reason:
- amplification incentives
- manipulation risk

---

## Events
Decay rapidly after event window.

Reason:
- temporary relevance

---

# Trust Acceleration

Trust should grow slowly.

Fast trust growth is dangerous.

Acceleration permitted only when:
- verification exists
- corroboration is consistent
- behavior remains stable over time

---

# Trust Collapse

Trust should collapse quickly when:
- coordinated abuse appears
- misinformation spreads
- spam bursts occur
- moderation actions escalate
- safety risks emerge

Trust systems should favor:
- protecting integrity

over:
- preserving inflated authority

---

# Freeze State

Freeze prevents immediate movement.

Use when:
- evidence conflicts
- moderation is investigating
- manipulation is suspected
- safety risk exists

Freeze applies to:
- visibility
- boosts
- amplification
- trust movement

Freeze is temporary.

---

# Recovery Rules

Recovery must be slower than collapse.

Recovery requires:
- sustained good behavior
- accurate updates
- low dispute rate
- moderation clearance
- successful verification

One good action should NOT erase repeated abuse.

---

# Contextual Trust

Trust is category-specific.

Examples:
- queue trust
- location trust
- timing trust
- occupancy trust
- event accuracy trust
- abuse risk trust

No single score should fully replace subscores.

---

# Trust and Ranking

Trust modifies:
- visibility persistence
- decay speed
- dispute tolerance
- boost efficiency
- suppression thresholds

Trust does NOT override:
- freshness
- safety
- verified contradiction

---

# Trust and Boosts

High trust:
- slightly improves boost efficiency
- delays saturation slightly

Low trust:
- reduces boost effectiveness
- increases saturation penalties
- increases cooldowns

Boosts cannot manufacture trust.

---

# Trust and Moderation

Moderation actions affect trust immediately.

Examples:
- verified abuse
- repeated disputes
- manipulation campaigns
- safety violations

All trust mutations must:
- be logged
- be explainable internally
- support appeals

---

# Trust Drift Detection

Monitor for:
- abnormal trust inflation
- suspicious recovery speed
- coordinated corroboration
- trust farming patterns
- cluster manipulation

Trust systems require observability.

---

# Immutable Trust Rules

- Trust is earned
- Trust decays
- Trust is contextual
- Trust is reversible
- Trust cannot be bought
- Trust never overrides safety
- Trust never bypasses freshness
- Frozen trust must remain reviewable

---

# v1 Defaults

Use:
- conservative trust gains
- aggressive abuse penalties
- rapid spam suppression
- contextual trust models
- visible decay

Optimize for:
- truth
- integrity
- operational confidence

NOT rapid authority accumulation
