# Ranking Constitution

## Purpose

Ranking exists to maximize:
- truthful awareness
- realtime usefulness
- geographic readability
- trusted coordination
- safe movement through nightlife environments

Ranking does NOT exist to maximize:
- screen time
- rage
- addiction
- creator ego
- spam visibility
- artificial urgency

## Constitutional Order

Every ranking decision must follow:

1. Safety
2. Truth
3. Trust
4. Freshness
5. Readability
6. Relevance
7. Monetization

Lower-order priorities may NEVER override higher-order priorities.

## Core Ranking Inputs

### Positive Signals
- recency
- corroboration
- trust score
- momentum velocity
- local density relevance
- user proximity
- interaction quality
- verified venue state

### Negative Signals
- stale persistence
- duplicate saturation
- low-trust amplification
- spam velocity
- unreadable cluster density
- synthetic engagement patterns
- unresolved moderation risk

## Visibility Doctrine

Visibility is earned.

Visibility is:
- temporary
- decaying
- contextual
- trust-weighted
- geography-aware

No entity is entitled to permanent amplification.

## Freshness Doctrine

Realtime systems decay aggressively.

All ranking entities must decay continuously.

Required decay classes:
- ultra-live
- live
- recent
- cooling
- stale
- archival

## Density Doctrine

High-density environments must compress.

Compression mechanisms:
- clustering
- representative leaders
- duplicate suppression
- saturation throttles
- district normalization

## Monetization Constraints

Paid amplification:
- must be labeled
- must be bounded
- must decay
- must remain trust-capped
- must remain readability-capped

Sponsored visibility must NEVER:
- dominate districts
- suppress emergencies
- override trust
- override safety systems
- masquerade as organic momentum

## Anti-Manipulation Rules

The platform must resist:
- brigading
- fake check-ins
- replay amplification
- bot swarms
- synthetic momentum
- repetitive repost flooding
- location spoofing

## Quiet-State Rule

Truthful quiet is valid.

Do not:
- inject synthetic activity
- inflate weak signals
- manufacture urgency

## Explainability Requirement

Ranking systems must be:
- inspectable
- measurable
- thresholded
- observable
- reversible
- rate-limited
- auditable

## Failure Doctrine

If confidence falls:
- reduce amplification
- reduce certainty
- reduce ranking persistence
- require corroboration
- prefer suppression over hallucination
