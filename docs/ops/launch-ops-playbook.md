# HOTMESS Launch Ops Playbook

## Purpose

Define how HOTMESS launches districts without:
- synthetic density collapse
- spam amplification
- unreadable maps
- trust instability
- venue imbalance
- operational overload

Launch quality matters more than launch size.

---

# Launch Doctrine

HOTMESS launches district-first.

NOT:
- city-wide first
- country-wide first
- global-first

The objective is:
- believable density
- operational trust
- local usefulness
- moderation stability
- venue legitimacy

A small alive district is stronger than a dead city.

---

# Launch Order

## Phase 1 — Seed District

Target:
- one dense nightlife district

Examples:
- Soho
- Vauxhall
- Kreuzberg
- Bushwick

Requirements:
- venue density
- nightlife frequency
- promoter activity
- repeat social behavior
- walkable geography

---

## Phase 2 — Verified Core

Before public launch secure:
- 5–10 trusted venues
- 2–3 trusted promoters
- moderation coverage
- operational monitoring
- event cadence

The map must feel:
- locally useful
- trustworthy
- readable

NOT artificially inflated.

---

## Phase 3 — Controlled Density

Initial growth should be:
- invite-based
- geographically constrained
- operationally monitored

Do NOT optimize for:
- total installs
- broad geographic expansion
- vanity growth metrics

Optimize for:
- repeat opens
- trust
- signal usefulness
- venue reliability

---

# Density Seeding

## Allowed

- verified venue onboarding
- legitimate launch events
- founder-assisted activation
- scheduled district coordination
- trusted promoter partnerships

## Forbidden

- fake crowds
- fake check-ins
- fake queues
- synthetic urgency
- fake momentum
- bot density inflation

Truthful low-density is preferable to fake activity.

---

# District Selection Rules

A launch district should have:
- strong nightlife repetition
- recognizable geography
- venue concentration
- promoter overlap
- repeat weekly behavior
- safe moderation scope

Avoid districts that are:
- too geographically spread
- operationally fragmented
- culturally unstable
- low-frequency nightlife zones

---

# Venue Onboarding

Initial venues must:
- understand the product
- update reliably
- maintain operational accuracy
- participate consistently
- tolerate experimentation

Prioritize:
- cultural relevance
- reliability
- operational maturity

NOT:
- raw follower counts
- tourist popularity alone

---

# Promoter Onboarding

Promoters should:
- create repeat movement
- operate trusted events
- maintain accurate information
- avoid spam behavior

Promoters are amplification-sensitive actors.

Monitor aggressively.

---

# Moderation Readiness

Launch should not proceed without:
- active moderation coverage
- abuse escalation paths
- trust monitoring
- stale leakage monitoring
- saturation controls

Moderation readiness outranks growth readiness.

---

# Launch Metrics

Track:
- repeat opens
- trusted signal density
- stale leakage
- correction latency
- venue participation
- promoter concentration
- map readability
- saturation pressure
- moderation load

Do NOT treat raw installs as primary success metric.

---

# Early Saturation Controls

In early launch:
- aggressive clustering
- aggressive suppression
- strong repetition penalties
- conservative boosts
- conservative visibility caps

v1 should prioritize:
- integrity
- readability
- trust

NOT:
- maximum volume

---

# Quiet State Handling

Early districts may be quiet.

Do NOT:
- inflate density artificially
- exaggerate turnout
- simulate momentum

Instead:
- surface trusted venues
- surface upcoming events
- preserve truthful emptiness

Trust compounds.

Fake momentum destroys trust permanently.

---

# Expansion Gates

Do NOT expand to new districts until:
- moderation is stable
- trust metrics stabilize
- stale leakage remains low
- readability remains healthy
- promoter concentration is controlled
- operational correction latency is acceptable

Expansion readiness is operational.

NOT marketing-driven.

---

# Failure Conditions

Critical launch failures include:
- unreadable density
- fake urgency perception
- stale dominance
- promoter flooding
- sponsor overexposure
- moderation overload
- trust collapse

If detected:
- reduce amplification
- reduce expansion
- tighten caps
- slow onboarding
- increase moderation

---

# Operational Doctrine

HOTMESS should feel:
- alive
- trustworthy
- breathable
- useful
- local
- realtime

NOT:
- chaotic
- inflated
- spammy
- artificial
- overcrowded

---

# Immutable Launch Rules

- Safety outranks growth
- Truth outranks momentum
- Trust outranks monetization
- Readability outranks density
- Quiet states are valid
- Fake urgency is forbidden
- Boosts remain bounded
- Moderation readiness is mandatory
