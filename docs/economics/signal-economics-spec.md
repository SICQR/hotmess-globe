# Signal Economics Spec

## Purpose

Signal Economics defines the scarcity, lifecycle, amplification, and saturation rules for HOTMESS.

It exists to:
- preserve readability
- prevent spam inflation
- maintain scarcity
- support truthful realtime density
- bound monetization
- prevent amplification abuse

Signal economics is the operating physics of visibility.

---

# Core Principle

Signals must be:
- scarce enough to matter
- frequent enough to feel alive

Too many signals create:
- clutter
- spam
- distrust
- unreadable density

Too few signals create:
- dead maps
- weak retention
- low utility

The goal is controlled scarcity.

---

# Signal Types

## User Intent Signals
Examples:
- going out
- here now
- crew up
- heading there

Characteristics:
- short-lived
- low authority
- fast decay
- local relevance

---

## Venue State Signals
Examples:
- open
- queueing
- busy
- afterhours

Characteristics:
- higher authority
- operational truth
- slower decay
- stronger district relevance

---

## Event Signals
Examples:
- starting soon
- live now
- nearly full

Characteristics:
- time-bound
- momentum-sensitive
- district-visible

---

## Promoter Signals
Examples:
- featured tonight
- boosted lineup
- ticket release

Characteristics:
- monetizable
- tightly bounded
- saturation-limited

---

# Core Economic Rules

- Signals are finite
- Signals decay
- Visibility is bounded
- Boosts are constrained
- Quiet states are valid
- Trust modifies amplification
- Monetization cannot dominate readability

---

# Creation Rights

## Users
May:
- create personal intent signals
- update movement context

May not:
- impersonate venue truth
- flood districts
- bypass cooldowns

---

## Venues
May:
- update operational states
- publish venue truth
- update queue and occupancy

Subject to:
- verification
- moderation
- trust scoring

---

## Promoters
May:
- amplify events
- publish event updates
- purchase bounded boosts

May not:
- override venue truth
- fabricate urgency
- exceed saturation caps

---

# Durations

## User Signals
Default:
- 60–120 minutes

Max:
- 4 hours

---

## Venue Signals
Default:
- 2–4 hours

Max:
- until operational state changes

---

## Event Signals
Default:
- until event completion

---

## Boosts
Default:
- 30–90 minutes

Boosts should decay rapidly.

---

# Cooldowns

Cooldowns apply by:
- user
- signal type
- district
- venue
- promoter

Free users:
- longer cooldowns
- stricter repetition penalties

Trusted users:
- more flexibility
- bounded tolerance

Low-trust actors:
- harsher throttling
- stronger caps

---

# Saturation Rules

The system must cap:
- visible signals
- repeated boosts
- district dominance
- category repetition
- promoter flooding

Districts should remain:
- balanced
- legible
- multi-source

---

# Boost Mechanics

Boosts may:
- improve prominence slightly
- increase temporary visibility
- help important events cut through noise

Boosts may NOT:
- override trust
- override safety
- dominate districts indefinitely
- create fake activity
- bypass readability constraints

Boosts must:
- decay quickly
- saturate aggressively
- remain bounded

---

# Trust Interaction

High trust:
- improves persistence
- improves boost efficiency slightly
- softens cooldowns

Low trust:
- accelerates decay
- reduces amplification
- increases throttling
- increases suppression likelihood

Trust affects economics directly.

---

# Lifecycle

Signals move through:
- created
- active
- decaying
- stale
- expired
- removed

Signals should visibly weaken over time.

---

# Quiet-State Economics

Low activity is truthful state.

The system must NEVER:
- fabricate density
- fake urgency
- simulate activity

When quiet:
- verified venues gain relative weight
- upcoming events remain visible
- honest emptiness remains valid

---

# Anti-Inflation Rules

Prevent:
- endless reposting
- spam amplification
- permanent boosts
- duplicate flooding
- fake urgency loops

The system should:
- shorten abusive durations
- increase cooldowns
- reduce visibility
- collapse repetition

---

# Readability Protection

Readability is an economic constraint.

If density becomes unreadable:
- collapse clusters
- suppress duplicates
- hide low-value repetition
- preserve strongest representatives

Monetization never overrides clarity.

---

# Immutable Invariants

- Signals are finite
- Signals decay
- Visibility must be earned
- Boosts are bounded
- Trust outranks payment
- Readability outranks monetization
- Quiet states are valid
- Stale data must not appear live
